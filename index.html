The Dressmaker

The story follows a woman who returns to her small Australian hometown years after being forced to leave as a child. Back then, she was accused of murdering a boy, and the mayor and police drove her out of town. She grew up believing she was a bad person, even though she barely remembered what happened. Now an expert dressmaker, she comes back to the town to uncover the truth and get revenge on those who wronged her. She returns to her childhood home, where her mother, now bitter and mentally unstable, refuses to welcome her. Despite this, she stays to help her mother and piece together what really happened on the day of the boy’s death. The townspeople, a small group of nosy and judgmental people, are shocked to see her again. They still believe she’s a murderer, but when they learn about her talent for making stunning dresses, their curiosity turns to admiration. As she transforms their appearances with her creations, she also gathers clues about her past. Little by little, her memories return. She learns that the mayor, the man who sent her away, is her real father—and also the father of the boy she was accused of killing. Eventually, she discovers the truth: the boy’s death was an accident, and a teacher lied about her involvement to give the mayor an excuse to get rid of her. Along the way, she falls in love, but her happiness is short-lived when her lover dies. Heartbroken but determined, she finishes her plan for revenge. She creates beautiful dresses for everyone in town to wear to a big event, and while they’re away, she burns the town down. In the end, she leaves the ashes behind, free from her past and the lies that haunted her.
